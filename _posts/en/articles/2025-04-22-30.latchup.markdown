# Single Event Latch-Up (SEL)

SEL differs in that its trigger is ionâ€‘induced charge. Because the initial conditions of SEL are much harsher than those of an external voltage transient, even devices that are robust against general latchâ€‘up require separate verification for SEL.

# Turn-on sequence of the parasitic PNPN structure induced by heavy ions

For example, when a heavy ion or other highâ€‘energy particle traverses the nâ€‘well, electronâ€“hole pairs are generated along the strike track instantaneously (picosecondsâ€“nanoseconds). Immediately after generation, the charges are collected by drift under the junction electric field: electrons move mainly toward the nâ€‘well, and holes move mainly toward the pâ€‘substrate. This injected current produces voltage drops (Î”ğ‘‰=ğ¼â‹…ğ‘…) across the nâ€‘well resistance R<sub>Nwell</sub> and the pâ€‘substrate resistance R<sub>Psub</sub>, respectively. <br>
While the majority carriers (electrons) in the nâ€‘well are being collected into the nâ€‘well tap (an n+ well contact tied to VDD), the voltage drop across R<sub>Nwell</sub> lowers the local nâ€‘well (base) potential relative to VDD. As the potential difference becomes large enough to forwardâ€‘bias the P<sup>+</sup>â€“nâ€‘well junction (the Eâ€“B of the parasitic vertical PNP), the PNP turns on first, injecting holes from P<sup>+</sup> (emitter) toward the pâ€‘substrate (collector). *(The PMOS P<sup>+</sup> source/drain serves as the emitter of this PNP.)* <br>
The hole current that flows into the pâ€‘substrate (base) exits through the p<sup>+</sup> substrate tap (tied to VSS), creating an additional voltage drop across R<sub>Psub</sub>. When the pâ€‘substrate (base) potential rises above that of the local n<sup>+</sup> (emitter, near VSS), the Eâ€“B of the parasitic lateral NPN becomes forwardâ€‘biased and the NPN turns on. Electrons are injected from the n<sup>+</sup> emitter into the pâ€‘substrate (base) and collected by the nâ€‘well (collector) *(the observed conventional current flows from the collector (nâ€‘well) to the emitter (n<sup>+</sup>))*.
This increases the nâ€‘well current and the voltage drop across R<sub>Nwell</sub>, which in turn further forwardâ€‘biases the PNP, forming a regenerative feedback loop. <br>

> The device that turns on first may be PNP, or NPN depending on conditions (LET/trajectory of the ion, strike location, tap density, etc.). In either case, the end result is that
> the parasitic PNPN structure is activated.

# Factors affecting the Turn on of the parasitic PNPN structure

<ul class="qrt-list">

<li>Particle physics (primary factor): The ion species, LET (linear energy transfer), and trajectory determine the amount and spatial distribution of charge generated in the sensitive volume. Higher LET injects more charge, increasing the probability of SEL. Larger induced current leads to a larger induced voltage, raising the likelihood of forming a forward bias across the Eâ€“B junction.</li>

<li>Doping/resistance: Lower substrate/well doping increases the sheet resistance, so the same charge creates a larger Î”V, reducing the critical charge for turnâ€‘on.</li>

<li>Guard ring/tap spacing: The farther the strike point is from the nearest tap, the higher the path resistance, making it easier for the parasitic BJTs to turn on.</li>

<li>Operating voltage: As V<sub>DD</sub> increases, the voltage across each resistance increases, making SEL possible with less charge.</li>

<li>Temperature: Higher temperature generally reduces the BJT turnâ€‘on voltage and increases Î² (bipolar gain), which accelerates turnâ€‘on and increases the gain product, thereby raising the likelihood of SEL.</li>
</ul>

SEL is a catastrophic error that cannot be cleared until power is removed. Even when not externally visible, metal melting, microâ€‘cracks, and electromigration can occur as latent damage, leading to lifetime degradation. In severe cases, thermal runaway due to large current can destroy the chip.
Therefore, for devices operated in radiationâ€‘exposed environments such as space, aviation, accelerators, and high altitude, SEL evaluation is essential. Moreover, general latchâ€‘up robustness does not guarantee SEL robustness, so heavyâ€‘ion evaluation is required.
