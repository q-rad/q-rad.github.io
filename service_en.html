What is Up-Screening?


Up-Screening is the process of screening and characterizing commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) electronic components to high-reliability levels tailored to customer needs. Through evaluations that reflect real operating environments—high/low temperature, vibration, electrical stress, and radiation—the performance and stability of components can be verified in advance. This allows reliable use of cost-effective industrial-grade parts instead of expensive military or space-grade components, achieving both cost savings and shorter lead times.

External Visual Inspection

External Visual Inspection is a basic procedure to check a component’s surface, markings, and leads for any abnormalities. In accordance with ESCC 2055000 and 2059000, 10 samples are inspected and defects are recorded.
Microscope at QRT

X-ray

X-ray inspection non-destructively examines the internal structure and is performed on 100% of parts. Per MIL-STD-750 and MIL-STD-883, radiation dose must be limited so as not to affect part reliability.
X-ray Inspection at QRT

C-SAM

C-SAM is a non-destructive method to detect internal delamination, voids, etc., in plastic packages. It is performed after electrical testing and before preconditioning, in accordance with JEDEC J-STD-020.
SAM (Scanning Acoustic Tomography) at QRT

Initial Electrical Test

The Initial Electrical Test verifies that functions and electrical characteristics are normal before reliability testing. Conducted at 25°C on all units, with certain parameters recorded quantitatively for comparison with post-test results.
Parameter Analyzer at QRT

Mechanical Shocks

Evaluates whether parts can withstand mechanical shocks from drops, impacts, and transportation without physical damage. The specimen is not struck directly but is subjected to a sudden change in acceleration that imposes stress. For example, shock waves from opening and closing a door are transmitted to components mounted inside the door—this transient acceleration is classified as mechanical shock.
Mechanical Shock Test at QRT

Vibrations

Confirms that components remain structurally and functionally sound under vibrations during transport or equipment operation. An electrodynamic shaker generates the intended amplitude, frequency, and acceleration. Sine vibration is used to find resonant frequencies, while random vibration emulates real-world environments.
BLR Vibration at QRT

Constant Acceleration

Assesses the physical effects of centrifugal-force stress on cavity devices. Designed to reveal structural and mechanical weaknesses not always detected by shock or vibration tests, this accelerated test can apply high stress levels as a destructive evaluation to establish mechanical limits of the package, internal metals and leads, and die-to-substrate interfaces. With appropriate stress settings, it can also be used to screen out parts with lower-than-normal mechanical robustness.
Constant Acceleration at QRT

Preconditioning

Preconditioning reproduces the handling and assembly steps from device shipment to PCB mounting and soldering, verifying that the device operates normally after soldering. In other words, it aims to screen device defects before board-level product shipment. The image shows equipment for moisture-related reliability pre-treatments during preconditioning and for delamination/crack assessments through high-temperature soldering processes.
Moisture Sensitivity Level Classification at QRT

Highly Accelerated Stress Test

Electronics often operate in high-temperature, high-humidity conditions. If sealing is insufficient, internal metal features can suffer corrosion or migration; THB testing evaluates resistance to these failure mechanisms. This test confirms that plastic-packaged parts maintain performance under hot/humid conditions. After preconditioning, perform either 96-hour HAST or 1000-hour THB, and there must be no functional anomalies before or after testing.
Highly Accelerated Stress Test at QRT

Thermal Cycling

Thermal Cycling evaluates endurance to repeated extreme temperature changes (–55°C ~ +125°C). It aims to detect defects caused by thermal expansion/contraction in real use and must include preconditioning. A total of 500 cycles are performed, with physical/functional checks via electrical testing at 25°C.
Temperature Cycling at QRT

Lifetest

Lifetest operates parts at high temperature for 2000+ hours to confirm that function/performance is maintained over time. It is essential for long-term reliability assurance and lifetime prediction, with electrical characterization per MIL standards.
Highly Accelerated Stress Test at QRT

Burn-in

Burn-in applies thermal and electrical stress to precipitate and remove early failures. Conditions may be selected from 125°C/240 h, 105°C/445 h, or 85°C/885 h, and testing is performed per MIL-STD-750/883. Depending on product specs, consider temperature limits and activation energy (Ea); post burn-in anti-oxidation and other treatments are also required.
Burn-in Test (HTOL) at QRT

Internal Visual Inspection

Internal Visual Inspection directly observes internal structures (die, bonding, leadframe, etc.) to assess manufacturing quality and anomalies—one of the core steps of Destructive Physical Analysis (DPA). It is performed for reliability assurance and counterfeit screening. Sample preparation is required to partially or fully remove the encapsulant protecting the die without damaging wires and bonding pads. QRT provides de-capsulation not only for EMC but also for devices with glass or metal packages.
De-capsulation at QRT

Die & Bond shear test

Evaluates durability against mechanical shear stresses that can occur during manufacturing, handling, testing, transport, and end use. Wire bond shear and pull tests are conducted after epoxy removal in the DECAP process. Solder Ball Shear applies to all surface-mount packages except flip-chip and is performed prior to PCB assembly. Each solder ball is sheared individually, and shear stress and failure modes are collected and analyzed.
Bonding Test System at QRT

SEM inspection

SEM inspection refers to Scanning Electron Microscopy used in DPA and quality-assurance workflows. It includes checking the condition of metal wires between chip and package leads; inspecting for cracks, missing wires, and contamination; verifying the integrity of the glassivation (passivation) layer; and examining interconnects and metal patterns for cracks, deformation, and porosity.
SEM/EDS Analysis at QRT

Glassivation integrity

Verifies the quality of the dielectric protective layer (glassivation) formed over metal interconnects. By chemical etching to expose the metal without damage, we confirm whether the protection layer is intact and the etch conditions are appropriate.
TEM/EDS/EELS Analysis at QRT

Lead finish analysis and pure tin identification

Lead Finish Analysis and Pure Tin Identification determine the surface finish composition of component leads and assess RoHS compliance. We analyze which metals are used in the plated layers—typically combinations of Sn, Pb, Ni, Au, and Cu. Pure tin finishes can grow filament-like whiskers over time, potentially causing shorts; thus, in high-reliability space/aerospace/defense applications, identifying pure tin in lead-free (RoHS) processes is critical.
Tin Whisker Acceptance at QRT

Total Ionizing Dose

Total Ionizing Dose (TID) is the cumulative energy absorbed when equipment or components are exposed to radiation (gamma, X-ray, electron, etc.) over time. Devices may experience gradual performance degradation or malfunction; TID testing evaluates resistance to these effects. QRT performs TID testing using domestic gamma facilities.
Total Ionizing Dose

Displacement Damage Dose

Displacement Damage (DD) occurs within the semiconductor bulk—unlike TID, which is limited to surfaces or interfaces—and affects the entire device volume. It can change electrical, optical, and thermal properties, leading to performance loss or failure. QRT conducts DD testing using domestic proton facilities.
Displacement Damage Dose

Single Event Effects - Pulsed Laser

Pulsed Laser SEE Test (PLSEE) uses femtosecond pulsed lasers to emulate the effects of space radiation on semiconductor devices, serving as an alternative to traditional radiation testing. QRT is the only company domestically with many years of laser-based radiation evaluation experience. Using a femtosecond laser system, we implement both Single Photon Absorption (SPA) and Two Photon Absorption (TPA) methods. At QRT, you can conduct laser-based radiation substitution evaluations and perform design verification for radiation-hardened semiconductors.
Laser evaluation system at QRT

Single Event Effects - Heavy Ion

Single Event Effects (SEE) arise when radiation (protons, neutrons, heavy ions, gamma rays, etc.) ionizes matter while passing through a semiconductor, inducing functional errors in circuits. If the electron–hole pairs generated by ionization reach sensitive regions in sufficient quantity to change operating states, errors occur. Most SEEs are non-destructive and transient; normal operation resumes after resetting values or power.
